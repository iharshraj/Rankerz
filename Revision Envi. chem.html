    • Atmospheric Pollution



  • We will mainly study Tropospheric and Stratospheric pollutions.

    • Tropospheric Pollution

  • Troposphere suffers from two types of pollutants , Gaseous and particulate pollutants.

  • Gaseous Pollutants : They could be oxides of suphur , Nitrogen or carbon ;Hydeogen sulphides , hydrocarbons , ozones etc
    Particulate Pollutants : They are fine particles mixed in air , eg Dust , fumes, mist.

  • Gaseous Pollutants:-
    (a) Oxides of Sulphur : • They are produced when sulphur cointaining fossil fuels are burnt.
       • SO2 is poisioneous to plants and animals, it causes asthma, bronchitis and emphysema ,SO2 and SO3 causes irritation to eyes.
       • In presence of Particulate Matters (P.M.) SO2 gets oxidised to SO3.
            SO2(g) + O2(g)     P.M.    > SO3(g)
            SO2(g) + O3(g)     P.M.    > SO3(g)
            SO2(g) + H2O2(l)     P.M.    > H2SO4(l)

    (b) Oxides of Nitrogen : N2 + O2     lightening    > NO2
            N2 + O2   1487K   > NO (reactive)
            NO + O2               > NO2
            NO + O3               > NO2 (faster)
       • NO2 oxidises to NO3- ,which acts like fertilizer.
       • NO and NO2 causes red haze in traffic.
       • NO2 retards photosynthesis.

    (c) Hydrocarbons : • They are carcinogenic (cause cancer).
       • Hydrocarbons formed due to incomplete combustion are poisoneous.
       • They cause ageing in plants and shedding of leaves.

    (d) Oxides of Carbon : • CO is colorless and odorless and highly poisoneous, they bind with haemoglobin complex
          and blocks oxygen. It causes premature birth , abortion and deformed babies.
       • CO2 causes global warming.

  • Global Warming : CO2 is transparent for sunlight but they trap infrared rays causing in increase of temperature
    CFC , methane , N2O (laughing gas) are also potent gases.

  • Acid Rain : Acid rain have a PH < 5.6 , CO2 SO2 and NO2 change in their respective acid forms to cause acid rain.
    Ammonium salts are also observed as haze (aerosols) during acid rain.

  • Particulate Pollution : They are fine particles as pollutants.
    They can be viable (living) or non-viable , generally humans cause non-viable one.
    non-viable pollutants are of 4 types
    (a) Smoke = It consists of solid or, solid+liquid particles , formed during combustion of organic materials.
    (b) Dust = Its made of small solid particles produced due to crushing or grinding of solid materials.
    (c) Mists = They are caused due to Condensation of vapors in air (eg vapors of water , sulphuric acid , insecticides , herbicides etc)
    (d) Fumes = Condesation of organic solvents or Metallic oxide vapors during sublimation.

    • Smog

  • Smog : Smoke + Fog , they represent pollution.
    (a) Classical Smog : • It occurs in cool and humid weather (during winters).
    • Its made of , smoke + fog + SO2.
    • Its reducing in nature.
    • Its also called , Reducing smog ,london smog ,and sulphurous smog.

    (b) Photochemical Smog : • It occurs in hot and dry weather (during summers).
    • Its made of , smoke + fog + NO2 + CxHy + O3 + PAN + acetone + Formaldehyde + acrolein.
    • CxHy here is unsaturated hydrocarbons produced due to incomplete combustion inside vehicles.
        (Vehicles also produce Nitorgen Oxide , NO) , and PAN = Peroxy-Acetyl-Nitrate.
    • Its Oxidising in nature.
    • Its also called Los angles Smog , and summer smog.

    • Mechanism for Photochemical smog

  • Cars mainly release NO + CxHy (unsaturated)
  • When conc of NO is increased (faster in presence if O3) it converts into NO2
    NO              > NO2   Sunlight  > NO + [O]
    [O]     O2    > O3     NO    > NO2
    This increases conc of (NO2 + O3) which is a very good Oxidising Agent (O.A)

  • Now , the O.A above will oxidise the unburnt unsaturated hydrocarbon ,
    CxHy (or, R'-CH3)     O3    > R-Ċ=O     O2    > R-CO-O-Ȯ
    R-CO-O-Ȯ     NO2    > R-CO-O-O-NO2 [PAN , Peroxy-Acetyl-Nitrate]
    In short , CxHy     (NO2 + O3)    > PAN

  • CH4 + O3     hv    > CH2=O (Formaldehyde) + H2O , these hydrocarbon reactions also produces
    Acrolein (CH2=CH-CH=O) and , Acetone (CH3-CO-CH3)

    • Water Pollution


  • Pathogens : Disease carring agents are called pathogens.
    Human excreta cointains "Escherichia coli" and "Streptococcus Faecalis"

  • Organic Wastes , dont cause disease directly but they support life of bacterias and other beings
    More the organic waste , more microbs and aquatic plants => more oxidation/digestion of waste => depletion of Oxygen ,

  • This meter for Consumption of Oxygen shows how pure water is called BOD (Biochemical Oxygen Demand),
    More BOD => More Impure Water
    BOD ≤ 5ppm , for drinking water
    BOD ≥ 17ppm , its very polluted

  • There should be a min amount of DO (Dissolved Oxygen) for aquatic life to sustain
    DO < 6ppm , fishes will die

  • If DO is low ,bacteria will start anerobic processes which produces foul smell and harmful chemicals.

    • Chemical Pollutants

  • Earlier PCB (Poly-Chlorinated-Biphenyls) was used as cleaning solvents and many more industrial processes
    but it was found to be carcinogenic and also pollutes ground water.

  • Uses of fertilizers promotes algae growth in water bodies which depletes DO in it.
    The process of elimination of animal life in nutrient rich water bodies, due to plants life using up all DO
    is called Eutrophication.

    • International Standards for drinking Water

  • Floride : [F-] ≤ 1ppm , it makes teeth enamel stronger but at over 2ppm it causes brown mottling of teeth.

  • Lead : [Pb2+] ≤ 50 ppb , its highly toxic can cause kidney and liver failure.

  • Sulphate : [SO42-] < 500ppm , more than that and it can cause laxative effect.

  • Nitrate : [NO3-] < 50ppm , more than that it can cause "Blue Baby Syndrome" (methemoglobinemia)

  • Other Metals : [Cd] < 0.005ppm , [Mn] < 0.05ppm
    [Fe] < 0.2ppm , [Al] < 0.2ppm
    [Cu] < 3ppm , [Zn] < 5ppm
